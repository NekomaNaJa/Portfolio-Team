Cristiano Ronaldo, born February 5, 1985, is a legendary Portuguese footballer known for his goal-scoring, athleticism, and multiple Ballon d'Or wins.

He’s played for top clubs like Manchester United, Real Madrid, and Juventus.


Academic history

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, had limited formal education due to his early focus on football. At the age of 12, he moved to Lisbon to join Sporting CP's youth academy, leaving traditional schooling behind to pursue his football career. minimal academic background, Ronaldo’s dedication to football led him to become one of the greatest players in history.


Club career


Sporting CP (2002–2003)

Ronaldo began his professional career at Sporting CP in Portugal, where he showcased his skills as a young winger, catching the attention of top European clubs.

Manchester United (2003–2009)

At Manchester United, Ronaldo developed into a world-class player, winning three Premier League titles, one UEFA Champions League, and his first Ballon d'Or in 2008.

Real Madrid (2009–2018)

Ronaldo became Real Madrid's all-time leading scorer, winning four Champions League titles and four Ballon d'Or awards during this period.

Juventus (2018–2021)

Ronaldo joined Juventus, where he won two Serie A titles and was a key player, contributing significantly to the team's domestic success.

Manchester United (2021–2022)

Ronaldo returned to Manchester United, continuing to deliver strong performances, including several match-winning goals.

Al-Nassr (2023–Present)

Ronaldo currently plays for Al-Nassr in the Saudi Pro League, where he continues to break records and expand his influence in football globally.
